How we design for Kingman conditions

Below are some of the engineering and planning considerations that go into a typical Kingman outdoor living project. The overview stays simple on the surface—details are here when you want them.

We begin by understanding your property, your inspiration, and how you want to use the space. For Kingman homeowners, a major priority is proper drainage, especially in recently developed communities where construction standards often leave yards vulnerable during monsoon season.

Elevation mapping & drainage analysis

Using a professional transit/transom level, we measure the exact elevations across your yard to determine how water actually moves through the space.

In Kingman’s newer master-planned neighborhoods, many lots are:

  • Extremely flat (minimal natural fall)
  • Fully enclosed by block walls

These conditions commonly create standing water after storms, negative fall toward the home, trapped drainage behind block walls, and erosion channels along fence lines. During monsoon events, these issues become amplified, causing sudden flooding or long-term soil saturation.

Our elevation mapping allows us to identify low points, engineer proper water flow, integrate drains, swales, or grading adjustments, and ensure your landscape design is functional—not just visually appealing. This drainage-first approach is one of the key reasons Kingman homeowners choose Sanctuary Outdoor over traditional landscapers and builders.

Kingman and the surrounding high-desert communities experience unpredictable straight-line winds and sudden microbursts that can place extreme stress on outdoor structures. Because these events occur without warning, structural safety becomes just as important as aesthetics.

At Sanctuary Outdoor, every pergola, gazebo, and patio bar is designed with high-wind performance in mind—even when the item does not require a building permit.

Our wind-resilience standards include:

  • Engineered footings sized for local wind loads
  • Structural post anchoring using high-grade connectors and fasteners
  • Selection of products with verified high-wind ratings
  • Installation practices that meet or exceed local codes and ordinances

Our goal is simple: design structures that remain beautiful, functional, and safe—even during severe wind events common in this region.

Our work in Kingman follows a dual-phase visualization and documentation process—3D concepts for clarity and 2D architectural plans for precision.

Phase 1: 3D concept design

We create photo-realistic 3D images giving you a true visual preview of your future outdoor space. You’ll see materials, textures, lighting, spatial flow, and how each element relates to your home and views before any construction begins.

This is where we explore alternate layouts, pool and spa locations, pergola configurations, planting concepts, and lighting strategies—visually, not just on paper.

Phase 2: Architectural 2D plans

Once the 3D concept is approved, we develop architectural-grade 2D drawings using professional drafting software. These plans include proportional layouts, linear dimensions, area calculations, construction notes, and material callouts.

The 2D plans are used to dial in accurate pricing, guide crews during construction, and form the basis of building permit submissions when required.
