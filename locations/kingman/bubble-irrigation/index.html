What is bubble irrigation?

Bubble irrigation delivers higher water volume (typically 2+ gallons per minute) directly to tree and shrub root zones. Unlike drip irrigation which provides slow, steady delivery, bubblers create localized flooding that saturates the soil quickly—ideal for establishing young trees and watering large shrubs in desert-appropriate watering solutions.

Why Bubble Irrigation Matters in Kingman and Northwest Arizona

Bubble irrigation systems provide the right balance between water-efficient irrigation and the volume needed for trees and large shrubs in Arizona soil conditions.

Benefits of Bubble Irrigation

Efficient for trees: Excellent for watering young trees during establishment, as water is concentrated near the root ball for deep root watering.

Water conservation: Reduces water waste compared to spray sprinklers by minimizing overspray and evaporation reduction.

Deep saturation: Encourages deep rooting by allowing water to saturate soil at higher volume per minute than drip irrigation systems.

Versatility: Adjustable heads control flow rates for different clay soil watering or sandy soil irrigation needs.

When to Use Bubble Irrigation

  • Newly planted trees needing to establish roots
  • Large, thirsty trees or shrubs not in grass zones
  • Areas with hard, compacted soil where water needs to pond and sink deep
  • Replacement for sprinkler heads in zones converted from grass to shrub/tree beds
  • Properties with caliche considerations requiring deep water penetration

Bubble Irrigation Technical Details

Understanding the components and considerations for effective bubble irrigation in northwest Arizona.

Adjustable Bubblers

Allow users to adjust flow rate or spray pattern (stream or fan) for different plant needs and soil type considerations.

Pressure Compensating (PC) Bubblers

Maintain consistent flow rate even with water pressure fluctuations—essential for properties with varying elevation or pressure management challenges.

Fixed Flow Bubblers

Deliver steady, set amount of water—simple, reliable option for uniform tree watering needs.

Mini Bubblers

Lower flow rate option (connects to 1/4" line) that bridges the gap between drip emitters and full bubblers for medium-sized plants.

Volume & Speed

Bubblers: Deliver 2+ gallons per minute for quick saturation
Drip: Slow delivery at 0.5-2 gallons per hour for precise water application

Best Applications

Bubblers: Established or establishing trees, large shrubs needing deep saturation
Drip: Rows of plants, vegetable gardens, low-maintenance irrigation design

Soil Compatibility

Bubblers: Work best in clay soil that can handle high-density water delivery
Drip: Highly efficient in almost all Arizona soil conditions

What We Include

  • Pressure regulator: Essential for maintaining proper PSI and preventing damage
  • Filtration system: Screen or disc filters to prevent clogging prevention
  • Backflow preventer: Protects water supply from contamination (required by code)
  • Zone control: Separate valves for different plant types and watering needs
  • Irrigation controller: Automated watering systems with seasonal adjustments
  • System testing: Verify flow rates, coverage, and proper drainage after installation

Potential Overwatering

Bubblers can flood areas if not managed properly. We design systems with proper run times and zone control to prevent standing water and soil saturation issues.

Root Zone Coverage

As trees grow, single bubblers may not water the entire root zone. We plan for future growth and can add additional emitters as needed.

Regular Maintenance

Check for clogs, leaks, and proper operation. We provide system maintenance schedules and can handle seasonal adjustments.
