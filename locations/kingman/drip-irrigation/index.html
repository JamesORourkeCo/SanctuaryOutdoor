What is drip irrigation?

Drip irrigation delivers water slowly and steadily (typically 0.5-2 gallons per hour) directly to plant root zones through emitters. This low-maintenance irrigation design minimizes evaporation, prevents runoff, and keeps foliage dry—ideal for desert plants, raised beds, row crops, and water conservation in Arizona soil conditions.

Why Drip Irrigation Matters in Kingman and Northwest Arizona

Drip irrigation is the most water-efficient irrigation method for desert climate irrigation, delivering up to 50% water savings compared to conventional sprinklers.

Benefits of Drip Irrigation

Water savings: Up to 50% more efficient than conventional sprinklers through evaporation reduction and precise water application.

Healthier plants: Keeps foliage dry, reducing fungal diseases and promoting stronger root development.

Weed reduction: Only targets plant root zones, leaving surrounding soil dry and preventing weed growth.

Versatility: Ideal for containers, raised beds, rows, shrubs, and mixed plantings with different water needs.

Key Technical Advantages

  • Pressure management: Systems operate at 15-30 PSI (vs. 50-70 PSI household pressure)
  • Soil compatibility: Works in sandy, clay, and caliche soils
  • Emitter spacing: 12" for sandy soils, 18" for loam, 24" for clay
  • Fertigation capable: Deliver fertilizer directly through the system
  • Surface or subsurface: Install on top or buried 1-14" deep
