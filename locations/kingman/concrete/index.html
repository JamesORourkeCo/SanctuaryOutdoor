Concrete planning for Kingman homes

We design concrete patios, walkways, and slabs to handle desert heat cycles and heavy loads, while keeping water moving away from your home and toward proper drainage.

We start with how the concrete will be used. A backyard patio has different load requirements than an RV pad or a concrete slab for a shed. This determines thickness, reinforcement, and base prep.
Concrete must be pitched to move water away from the home and set two inches below the stucco line. We use laser levels to keep water flowing in the right direction and avoid creating a dam.
We remove soil to make room for road base, then compact for a stable foundation. Concrete is not poured directly on soil. This step protects against cracking in Kingman heat cycles.
Depending on the job, we use rebar, wire mesh, fiber, or additives for strength. Concrete is a cement and aggregate mix that needs the right spec for long-term durability.
We install stamped concrete Kingman AZ, broom finish concrete, and stained concrete. When matching older pavers, stamped and stained finishes can recreate color and pattern without special-order delays.

Drainage-aware concrete design

Concrete should not trap water. We plan slab edges, grading, and drainage so monsoon runoff has a path out of the yard.

Many yards end up with wall-to-wall concrete that blocks drainage. When we see that risk, we recommend a drainage path or drain pipe under slabs to keep water moving.
