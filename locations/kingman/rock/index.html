Why “just dump a thick layer of rock” backfires

These are the real complaints Kingman homeowners tell us after a builder or contractor spreads rock too thick instead of preparing the soil underneath.

A) Hard to walk on

When rock is spread too thick, every step can feel like you sink a little—similar to carpet padding that’s too thick. Even worse, thick rock can hide ruts and low spots that become obvious after monsoons.

B) Weeds show up anyway

Even with fabric underneath, weeds appear. Rock deliveries contain fines (sand/smaller particles). If rock is spread too thick, fines can create dams that slow drainage and form pockets where plants can grow— especially where water stands.

C) Mushy spots after rain

We’ve seen people sink to their ankles in builder-installed rock yards. Usually this means the soil wasn’t compacted or there are low areas holding water under the rock.

D) Standing water for days

Low areas hidden under rock, incorrect slope, and too many fines can keep water from moving. In some cases, a targeted drain line under the rock can be a cost-effective repair versus full removal and re-grade.

E) Plastic showing through

When plastic/fabric becomes visible, it often points to uneven subgrade—an unwanted high spot or a thin area caused by an un-flat base under the rock.

Monsoon reality check

Water flows over ground and through rock. If the soil beneath wasn’t verified for slope and low spots, the next storm will find every mistake—fast.

How Sanctuary Outdoor installs rock the right way in Kingman

Decorative rock should be the finish, not the fix. We focus on drainage-first prep so your rock yard stays clean-looking and functional.

Our rule: measure and correct the soil surface first, then spread rock. We use a transit/laser level to verify pitch and eliminate hidden low areas before rock is installed. If needed, we compact the subgrade and recommend targeted drainage solutions that don’t require tearing the whole yard apart.

1) Measure slope & low spots

We confirm water will flow away from the home and won’t pool under future rock.

2) Grade correction

We don’t hide ruts or dips—we fix them so your yard looks good and performs.

3) Compaction where needed

We address soft subgrade so the yard doesn’t turn mushy after storms.

4) Rock install (right thickness)

Enough coverage to look finished—without creating a drainage problem.
