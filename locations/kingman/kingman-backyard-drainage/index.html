Why yards flood in Kingman

Flooding is usually a combination of surface flow, hard surfaces, and small grade changes that push water into the wrong place.

1) Flat or negative grading

When the yard is flat — or slopes toward your patio, wall, or foundation — water collects fast and has nowhere to go.

2) Monsoon storms

Monsoon downpours can overwhelm yards in minutes. The goal is to route water away from structures before it turns into standing water.

3) Hard surfaces concentrate runoff

Roof lines, patios, and paths funnel water into low spots. Without a defined exit path, it pools against walls and slabs.

4) Soil conditions reduce absorption

Compacted soil (and caliche in many areas) can limit infiltration — making surface flow management even more important.
